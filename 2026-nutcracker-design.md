For a class, we were a given a design problem to imagine you have a macadamia nut that you want to crack open by hand using a simple lever nut cracker. We were asked to calculate the necessary dimensions of the nutcracker and come up with a design to make this task feasible. We were also given the liberty to assume a very simple geometry for the nutcracker to make calculations easier.

Since we had to imagine a macadamia nut, the force required to break it was fixed within a certain range, which is a load of 222.18 +/- 18.54 kg. The average size of macadamia nuts vary between 13mm - 20mm. I therefore chose a force of 2350 N (239.8 Kg) and a size of 1.5 cm (15 mm)

I approached this question by first finding a reasonable input or grip force required to crack such a nut. I decided to model the problem with a total input force of 800N (400N on each handle). I labeled the dimensions by calling the length between the center of nut in the devive to the point of applied force as x cm, and the from the center of the nut to the pin or joint of the nutcracker as y cm. Knowing all the required forces and the dimensions of the nut, I was able to find a relation between my two unknown dimensions using a momentum equilibrium equation in the designated z diection.

Based on my calculations and using a reasonable assumed value of 19.5 cm for dimension y, I obtained 4 cm for dimension x, making my nutcracker about 23.5 cm long. This is a bit large for a nutcracker. The long ddimensions of the handles (19.5 cm) also make it hard to press down for objects with bigger diameters since these handles will be further apart. As such, this design does not seem to be very practical in reality.
